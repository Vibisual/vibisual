## What this policy adds

A code license does not grant rights to a project's name or branding.
This policy clarifies how the **"Vibisual" name and logo** may be used.

### You MAY

- Use the source code under the terms of Apache 2.0, including for
  commercial purposes.
- Refer to the original Vibisual project by name in a descriptive way
  (nominative fair use), for example:
  - "Built with Vibisual"
  - "Compatible with Vibisual"
  - "Based on Vibisual"
- Use unmodified screenshots of Vibisual in articles, talks, reviews,
  comparisons, and tutorials about Vibisual.

### You MAY NOT

- Use the name "Vibisual" or a confusingly similar name (e.g. "Vibisuel",
  "Vibisuals", "VibiVisual") to identify a fork, derivative work, or
  competing product.
- Use the Vibisual logo or visual identity on any product, service, or
  marketing material that is not the official Vibisual project.
- Imply that your fork or derivative is endorsed, sponsored, or
  affiliated with the Vibisual project.
- Register the Vibisual name, similar names, or the logo as your own
  trademark in any jurisdiction.

## Forks

Forks are welcome. When you publish a fork:

1. Choose a clearly different name for your fork.
2. Update the README, package metadata, and any visible branding to your
   own name.
3. You may continue to mention "originally based on Vibisual" in a
   descriptive way.

## Questions

For trademark licensing inquiries, partnerships, or to request explicit
permission for a use not covered above, please open an issue on the
project repository.
